KAFKA CENTENARY

BROCHURE AND POSTER

This was the second assignment for Digital Design I: Vector Design, in which we were tasked with creating a double-sided print piece commemorating the 100th anniversary of Franz Kafka’s death. One side of the layout functioned as a brochure, summarizing Kafka’s novella The Metamorphosis, while the other side served as a poster.

For the brochure, I designed three vector illustrations, each corresponding to a key moment in the story: the first depicts the family's initial, shocked reaction upon seeing Gregor transformed into an insect, the second shows the cleaning lady looking at Gregor with a mixture of pity and sadness, and the third portrays his sister in anger, scolding him after he frightens the family’s lodgers by exposing himself.

Each illustration was visually and thematically connected to a short passage from the story included in the layout, helping to reinforce the narrative through imagery.

The reverse side, which is the poster, features a more introspective scene: Gregor alone in his room, which has been converted into a storage space. Only the sofa and a few boxes remain, symbolizing the isolation and neglect he experiences near the end of the story.

This project required not only illustration and layout skills in Adobe Illustrator but also a thoughtful interpretation of literary content through visual design.
