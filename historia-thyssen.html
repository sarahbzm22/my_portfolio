VERSIONA THYSSEN XXI ENTRY

CHRIST THROUGH TIME

This digital illustration was created for both a university assignment and as a submission to the Versiona Thyssen XXI competition organized by the Museo Nacional Thyssen-Bornemisza. Participants were asked to reinterpret one of several selected artworks from the museum's collection. I chose The Resurrected Christ by Bramantino (Bartolomeo Suardi), and titled my version Christ Through Time.

Using Photoshop for both illustration and image editing, I preserved key elements of the original painting, such as Christ's pose and part of the surrounding composition, but reimagined the piece through the visual metaphor of a shattered mirror. Each shard reflects a different historical portrayal of Christ, drawing from a range of artistic periods and styles.

The fragments depict were: The Resurrected Christ by Bramantino, Young Christ by Bartolomeo Montagna, Christ in Majesty from the Aberdeen Bestiary and The Yellow Christ by Paul Gauguin

I intentionally left the center of the mirror broken and empty to symbolize the unreachable mystery of Christ's true nature. This void represents the idea that no single artistic representation can fully capture his essence, inviting viewers to reflect on the limitations of visual interpretation and the spiritual transcendence of the figure.

While each fragment presents a distinct version of Christ, the cracks between them serve as symbolic seams, binding diverse representations through a shared thread of artistic expression across time. Different colors, textures, and techniques were applied to each section to emphasize both contrast and unity, reinforcing the idea that despite varied depictions, the essence of Christ remains constant.
