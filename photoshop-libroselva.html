BOOK COVER DESIGN FOR

THE JUNGLE BOOK

In this project, which was part of the Digital Design II: Image Editing course, we focused on mastering Photoshop for creative design tasks. The assignment was to design a book cover for The Jungle Book, but with a twist: we were encouraged to steer clear of the typical Disney-inspired, child-friendly version of the story.

My approach to the cover design was to infuse it with a darker, more mysterious atmosphere, reflecting the true essence of Rudyard Kipling’s original work. I chose a color palette that leaned towards darker tones, with deep greens, blacks, and subtle highlights, creating an overall moody and enigmatic vibe.

The central visual element was a close-up of a black panther’s eye. This eye was meant to represent the jungle’s dangerous allure and its primal nature. In the reflection of the panther's eye, I portrayed Mowgli, the protagonist, navigating his way through the dense jungle foliage. This reflected the tension and complexity of his journey and the animals that shaped his destiny in the wild.

I chose an amphora as the type of ceramic vessel for this artwork. Amphorae were commonly used for storing food and items such as laurel leaves, which connects thematically to the myth and adds contextual logic to the choice of form.

Although it was a smaller-scale project without a detailed dossier required, it allowed me to experiment with different Photoshop techniques and challenge the idea of a familiar story by creating a visually striking and unique interpretation of The Jungle Book.
